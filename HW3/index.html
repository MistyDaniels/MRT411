You awaken to darkness. A blindfold blocks your vision, but by the jarring rocking motion you are aware of the fact youre seated within a moving cart. The air is crisp and you can hear the hushed whispers of others around you.

You remove the blindfold, but find that your wrists and ankles are shackled together. The commotion has alerted your companions of your regained awareness.

Ralof: Hey, you. You're finally awake. You were trying to cross the border, right? Walked right into that Imperial ambush, same as us, and that thief over there.

You glance to your side to see a skittish thief seated beside you.

Lokir: Damn you Stormcloaks. Skyrim was fine until you came along. Empire was nice and lazy. If they hadn't been looking for you, I could've stolen that horse and been half way to Hammerfell. You there. You and me -- we should be here. It's these Stormcloaks the Empire wants.

After a short conversation you arrive in a small village. The Imperials have decided your fate. You are sentenced to death.

Lokir the thief tries to flee when the lot is sentences to death. He is swiftly shot through with Imperial arrows.

You resign yourself to your fate with the Stormcloaks and place your head upon the slab.

A dull roar can be heard growing in the distance.

Alduin perches himself upon the spires roof, his weight shaking the earth beneath him.

His sudden appearance and the fire pouring from his mouth halt the execution, everyone flees for their life.


Ralof cuts your bonds and you make your escape to begin your journey as the Dragon Born.

You decide to leave your blindfold.

Whatever trouble you're already in isnt worth making worse.

The rest of the group keeps their voices down, too low for you to hear.

The cart stops and you are suddenly pulled from it.

Its difficult to determine what is going on, but someone is shouting orders. It sounds as though you are in a great deal of trouble.

Your adrenaline pumps in your ears.

Before things can escalate you break into a spring, still bound.

You run a few yards before Imperial arrows pierce your back.

You hear someone call you a coward before everything fades to black
